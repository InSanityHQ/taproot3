\section{Endosymbiotic Theory}
\label{sec:orgf015786}
Endosymbiotic theory states that organelles within our current
eukaryotic cells --- the mitochondria and chloroplasts --- are
originally prokaryotic cells in their own right. This is because they
divide independently through binary fission, and also contains circular
DNA that is independent of the main cell itself.
