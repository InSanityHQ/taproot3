\section{Realism:}
\label{sec:orge730b07}
\emph{A Raison de etat inspired international relations theory}

\begin{itemize}
\item Community of states exists in \textbf{anarchy}: no worldwide "911" event, and
there's no universal world policy
\item Thus, international politics is a \textbf{self-help} system; allies could
help but security is not guaranteed

\begin{itemize}
\item allies are like the friend in the locker room: they may help you
beat someone up, but they aren't gaurenteed to be there
\end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Power} is the key to maintain security

\begin{itemize}
\item you never know how much power you need to stay safe
\end{itemize}

\item \textbf{Security} is an act of re-balance: one state's security hurt the
security of other states
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Longer term factors are nearly invisible.}

Causality exists between long term and short term events => Short term
changes are caused therefore by longer term change while long term
events are hyped up by shorter events
