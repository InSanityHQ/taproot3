\section{Nation-States}
\label{sec:org1c7fb93}
Idea of a "nation" is actually a new thing => "group of people with a
common culture, sense of identity, and political aspirations" (no longer
monarch-soverignty based).

\begin{enumerate}
\item Strong monarchs began creating stronger national states w/ breaking
local lord power + consolidating governmental power
\item National identity fostered by the culture (artists, writers,
linguists)

\begin{itemize}
\item Lit woks helped defined national groups + foster sense of identity
\item Musicians also contributed by adding elements local music into
their composition
\item Dictionaries were compiled to the understanding of the local
culture
\end{itemize}

\item Ethnic groups began acquiring a \emph{common heritage} => shapes identity
=> strengthens the heritage =>
\href{KBhHIST201Nationalism.org}{KBhHIST201Nationalism}
\end{enumerate}

And boom! Nation-states.
\href{KBhHIST201Nationalism.org}{KBhHIST201Nationalism}-engendered
cultural states.
