\section{Gender and the Biological Sciences}
\label{sec:orgfc60532}
\begin{itemize}
\item Existance of alternative theory challenges the status quo
\item The status quo is the informant of whach questions are asked, what
hypotheses are investigated, and which data is ignored
\item Accepted theories could be false or "apparently falsified"
\item Some hypotheses are designed such that they may be falsified but hard
to be proven false
\item Feminist empiricism => knower's standpoint is irrelevant and objective
methods evalues theories' correctness
\item "Standpoint epistemologists" => credentials of knowledge depend on the
knower's situations
\item Because hypotheses are asked and built upon other agreed upon
hypothesis, knowledge exists and are tested in a "branched" structure

\begin{itemize}
\item Impossible to purify theory because theories are all jumped off of a
source
\item Hence, scientific method could fail at nodes where theories
previously not accepted or explored are not expanded upon
\end{itemize}

\item Could either make decisions blind to the context of theory generation
and hence a more restricted hypothesis or to be informed by it
\item Bias in theories mode blinded to other possibilities could only be
mitigated with a community as a whole
\end{itemize}
