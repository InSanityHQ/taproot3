\section{inner product space\hfill{}\textsc{def}}
\label{sec:orgd514b51}
\begin{quote}
An \emph{inner product space} is a vector space \(V\) along with an \href{KBrefInnerProduct.org}{inner product} on \(V\).

When \(V = \FF ^n\), assume the inner product is the Euclidean inner product
\[\begin{aligned}
  \langle (w_1, \ldots, w_n), (z_1, \ldots, z_n) \rangle = w_1 \overline{z_1} + \cdots w_n \overline{z_n}
  \end{aligned}\]
\end{quote}
