Axler5.36 Eigenspace

eigenspace, $E(\lambda, T)$def

Suppose $T \in \mathcal{L} (V)$ and $\lambda \in \mathbb{F}$. The eigenspace of $T$ corresponding to $\lambda$ denoted $E(\lambda, T)$, is defined by \[\begin{aligned} E(\lambda , T) = \onull(T-\lambda I) \end{aligned}\] In other words, $E(\lambda , T)$ is the set of all eigenvectors of $T$ corresponding to $\lambda$, along with the 0 vector.

results

$\lambda$ is an eigenvalue of $T$ iff $E(\lambda ,T) \neq \{0\}$
