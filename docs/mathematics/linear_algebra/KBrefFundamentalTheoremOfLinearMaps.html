Fundamental Theorem of Linear Maps!

Written by Exr0n

Intuition

In a linear map $T : V\to W$, the dimension of the domain $V$ is amount of stuff that you throw away (null space) plus the amount of stuff that does not get thrown away (the column space).

<>#definition Fundamental Theorem of Linear Mapsdef

Suppose $V$ is finite-dimensional and $T \in \mathcal L(V, W). Then \text{range }T is finite-dimensional and $$ \text{dim }V = \text{dim null }T + \text{dim range} T $$

See also dimension, null space, and range of a function.

AKA: Rank Nullity Theorem
