Invariant Subspaces

Written by Exr0n

Axler 3.Asource

invariant subspacedef

Suppose $T \in \mathcal L(V)$. A subpsace $U$ of $V$ is called invariant under $T$ if $u \in U$ implies $Tu \in U$.

intuit

A subspace $U$ is called invariant on $T$ if $T\big|_U$ is closed in $U$. (BUT it is not nessecarily an operator!) Aka the map is closed under the subspace.

results

finite dimensional subspaces of sufficiently large dimension (1 for $\mathbb F = \mathbb C$ and 2 for $\mathbb F = \mathbb R$)
