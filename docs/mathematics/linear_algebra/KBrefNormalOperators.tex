\section{Axler7.18 normal\hfill{}\textsc{def}}
\label{sec:orgb3ed46e}
Not to be confused with \href{KBrefNormal.org}{normal vectors}, which have \href{KBrefNorm.org}{norm} 1.
\begin{quote}
\begin{itemize}
\item An operator on an inner product space is called \emph{normal} if it commutes with its adjoint.
\item aka: \(T \in  \mathcal{L}(V)\) is \emph{normal} if
\end{itemize}
\[\begin{aligned}
  TT^* = T^*T
  \end{aligned}\]
\end{quote}
Every \href{KBrefSelfAdjointOperators.org}{self adjoint} operator is normal, because \(TT = TT\)
\section{results}

\subsection{Axler7.20 \(T\) is normal iff \(\lVert Tv \rVert = \lVert T^* v \rVert\) for all \(v\)}
\label{sec:orga636d36}
This implies that \(\onull T = \onull T^*\) for all normal operators \(T\)..
\subsection{Axler7.21 For \(T\) normal, \(T\) and \(T^*\) have the same eigenvectors}
\label{sec:org5db13d0}
\begin{quote}
And the corresponding eigenvalues are conjugates of one another.
\end{quote}
\subsection{Axler7.22 Normal operators have orthogonal eigenvectors}
\label{sec:orgf582cec}
\begin{quote}
Suppose \(T \in  \mathcal{L}(V)\) is normal. Then eigenvectors of \(T\) corresponding to disticnct eigenvalues are orthogonal.
\end{quote}
