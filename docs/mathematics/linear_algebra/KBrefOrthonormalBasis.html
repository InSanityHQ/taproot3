Orthonormal Basis

Axler6.27 orthonormal basisdef

An orthonormal basis of $V$ is an orthonormal list of vectors in $V$ that is also a basis of $V$.

Pretty self explanatory.

How do we find an orthonormal basis? see the

results

Axler6.28 orthonormal list of the right length is a basis

Because it's linearly independent, and linearly independent lists of the right length are bases (Axler2.39).

Axler6.30 vector as a linear combo of orthonormal basis

Suppose $e_1, \ldots, e_m$ is an orthonormal basis of $V$ and $v \in V$. Then, \[\begin{aligned} v = \langle v, e_1 \rangle e_1 + \cdots \langle v, e_n \rangle e_n \end{aligned}\] and

\[\begin{aligned} \lVert v \rVert ^2 = |\langle v, e_1 \rangle|^2 + \cdots + \lvert \langle v, e_n \rangle \rvert ^2 \end{aligned}\]

By taking the inner product of both sides of the equation \[\begin{aligned} v = a_1e_1 + \cdots + a_n e_n \end{aligned}\] with $e_j$ for each $e_j$. And also the Pythagorean theorem
