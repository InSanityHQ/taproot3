Problem 3

\[ \begin{aligned} a\cdot b &= 120 \\ b(a) &= \frac{a}{120} \\ p(a) &= 5\cdot 2a + 6\cdot 2b(a) \\ &= 10a + \frac{1440}{a} \\ p'(a) &= 10 - \frac{1440}{a^2} \\ p'(a_0) &= 0 \\ 0 &= 10 - \frac{1440}{a_0^2} \\ \frac{1440}{a_0^2} &= 10 \\ 1440 &= 10\cdot a_0^2 \\ 144 &= a_0^2 \\ a_0 &= {-12, 12} \\ a_0 \text{ has to be positive; therefore } a_0 \text{ is equal to } 12 \text{.}\\ p(a_0) &= p(12) = 10(12) + 12\cdot \frac{12}{120} \\ & 120 + \frac{12}{10} \\ &= 121 \frac{1}{5} \\ \end{aligned} \]

The least amount of money would be spent with a dimension of 12x10 ft. As for the most amount of money, as it is possible to create an enclosure with an area of \(120\) square feet and an infinitely long perimeter, it is impossible to reach an answer.
