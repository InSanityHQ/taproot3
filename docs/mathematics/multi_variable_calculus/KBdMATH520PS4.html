\((2)\): If the xy-angle, z-angle, and magnitude of a point in 3D space are represented by the variables \(\theta, \phi, l\), then the vector representation of the point will be equal to \(<\sin{\phi}\cdot\cos{\theta}, \sin{\phi}\cos{\theta}, \cos{\phi}>\cdot l\). Therefore, the answer to \((1)\) is \(<\frac{\pi}{4}, \frac{\pi}{4}, \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}>\).
