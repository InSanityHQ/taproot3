Matrices

1a

\begin{aligned} BA &= \begin{bmatrix} 58 & 64 \\ 139 & 154 \\ \end{bmatrix} \end{aligned} BA is not equal to AB because $AB \in \mathbb{R}^{3\times 3}$ whereas $BA \in \mathbb{R}^{2\times 2}$ so there's no way that $AB = BA$ could be true.

1b

\begin{aligned} det(BA) &= (58)(154) - (64)(139) \\ &= 36 \end{aligned}

1c

\begin{aligned} AB &= \begin{bmatrix} 39 & 54 & 69 \\ 49 & 68 & 87 \\ 59 & 82 & 109 \\ \end{bmatrix} \\ det(AB) &= (30)(68)(109) + (54)(87)(59) + (69)(49)(82) - (69)(68)(59) - (54)(49)(109) - (39)(87)(82) \\ &= 24 \\ \end{aligned}
