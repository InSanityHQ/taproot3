TR3

Derivatives of Composed Functions

When you have \[\begin{aligned} x(t) = \text{ something } y(x) = y(x(t)) \end{aligned}\]

then \[\begin{aligned} \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{\frac{dy}{dt}}{\frac{dx}{dt}} \end{aligned}\]

because

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{dy}{dt} = \frac{dy}{dx}\frac{dx}{dt} \end{aligned}\] by the chain rule.
