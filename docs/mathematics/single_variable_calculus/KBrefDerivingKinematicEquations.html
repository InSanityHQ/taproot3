Deriving Kinematic Equations

Given

\[\begin{aligned} \begin{matrix}a=\frac{dv}{dt} & v = \int a dt\\ v = \frac{dx}{dt} & x = \int v dt\end{matrix} \end{aligned}\]

Derive the kinematic equations for constant acceleration

\[\begin{aligned} v &= \int a dt &= at + C_v\\ x &= \int v dt = \int (at+C_v) dt &= \frac{1}{2}at^2 + C_v t + C_x \end{aligned}\]

Letting $x_0 = C_x$ and $v_0 = C_v$, \[\begin{aligned} x = x_0+v_0t+\frac{1}{2}a_0t^2 \end{aligned}\]
