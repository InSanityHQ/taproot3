The Dot Product

definitions

geometric

\[\begin{aligned} \vec{A} \cdot \vec{B} \triangleq | \vec{A} | |\vec{B}| \cos \theta \end{aligned}\]

algebraic

results (from the geometric definition)

distribution: $(\vec{a} + \vec{b}) \cdot \vec{c} = \vec{a} \cdot \vec{c} + \vec{b} \cdot \vec{c}$

Let $\theta$ equal the angle between $\vec{a} + \vec{b}$ and $\vec c$. Let $\gamma$ equal the angle between $\vec a$ and $\vec b$.

\[\begin{aligned} ( \vec{a} + \vec{b} ) \cdot \vec{c} &= | \vec{a} + \vec{b} | |c| \cos \theta \\ &=\sqrt{| \vec{a} | ^2 + | \vec{b} | ^2 - 2 | \vec{a}| |\vec{b}| \cos \gamma }|c| \cos \theta \\ &=\sqrt{| \vec{c} |^2 | \vec{a} | ^2 + | \vec{c} |^2| \vec{b} | ^2 - 2 | \vec{c} |^2| \vec{a}| |\vec{b}| \cos \gamma } \cos \theta \end{aligned}\]
