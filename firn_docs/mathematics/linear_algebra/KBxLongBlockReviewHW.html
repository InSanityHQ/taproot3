$ \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 2x \\ y \end{bmatrix} $ -> scales x component by 2.

$ \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} -y \\ x \end{bmatrix} $ -> rotate 90 counterclockwise

$ \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \to \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 3y \end{bmatrix} $ -> aligns to y axis then scales by three

  1. /Now let's try considering how these transformations are related to the determinant... Recall that we denote the determinant of A as |A| to suggest that the determinant somehow gives the size of the matrix. How might you represent the *size* of the above matrices? Of the identity?/

When you multiply any of these matrices together, or with the identity, what is the *size* of the new matrix? By the product of linear maps, the determinant of the two matrices multiplied together.
