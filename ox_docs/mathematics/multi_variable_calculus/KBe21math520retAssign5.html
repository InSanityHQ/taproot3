1 the parabola

A parabola (defined by a focus point and a directrix line) is the set of points \[\begin{aligned} \{ P : |FP| = \text{ distance from } P \text{ to } L\} \end{aligned}\]

\[\begin{aligned} r &= r(\theta) \\ &= d - r \cos \theta\\ r + r \cos \theta &= d\\ r (1+\cos \theta ) &= d\\ r &= \frac{d}{1+\cos \theta} \end{aligned}\]

2.4 degenerate ellipse

It is a circle when \(a^2 = b^2\), aka

\[\begin{aligned} \frac{d^2e^2}{\left(1-e^2\right)^2} &= \frac{d^2e^2}{1-e^2}\\ \left( 1-e^2 \right) ^2 &= 1-e^2\\ 1-e^2 &= 1\\ e^2 &= 0\\ e &= 0\\ \end{aligned}\]

You have to take the limit. You get \[\begin{aligned} x^2y^2 &= d^2e^2 &= r^2 \end{aligned}\] What could \(d\) possibly be for \(\lim\limits_{e \to 0} de \neq 0\)?

\[\begin{aligned} d &= \frac{n}{e}\\ \lim_{e \to 0} de &= \lim_{e \to 0} \frac{ne}{e}\\ \end{aligned}\]
