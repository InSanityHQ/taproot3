* Matrices
** 1a
\begin{aligned}
BA &= 
\begin{bmatrix}
58 & 64 \\
139 & 154 \\
\end{bmatrix}
\end{aligned}
BA is not equal to AB because $AB \in \mathbb{R}^{3\times 3}$ whereas $BA \in \mathbb{R}^{2\times 2}$ so there's no way that $AB = BA$ could be true.
** 1b
\begin{aligned}
det(BA) &= (58)(154) - (64)(139) \\
&= 36
\end{aligned}
